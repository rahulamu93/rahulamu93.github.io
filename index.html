About

I design and build control systems for inverter-fed electric drives — from theoretical formulation to full hardware validation — with a focus on making drives robust under the unpredictable conditions of real-world electric vehicle and industrial applications.

My PhD research at IIT Jodhpur centres on nonlinear control for electric motor drives under practical operating conditions: load disturbances, parameter variations, and speed transients. I have developed integral sliding mode controllers (ISMC, ASISMC, ASTISMC) for sensorless EV drivetrains and validated them experimentally on dSPACE (1202/1104) and OPAL-RT platforms under standard drive cycles (NEDC, UDDS). This work sits at the intersection of control theory and power electronics hardware — a combination I find compelling and that I believe is critical for the next generation of converter-dominated energy systems.

On the hardware side, I led the design and development of a 3.7 kW PMaSynRM motor drive system (industry-sponsored by Bharat Bijlee), covering gate driver design, DC-DC auxiliary power supplies, shunt-based current sensing, PCB layout, and full system integration with real-time control on STM32F407. I also contributed to establishing the electric drive laboratory at IIT Jodhpur, building complete experimental setups including inverters, sensing systems, and motor drive platforms from scratch.

In parallel, I work as a Senior Motor Control Engineer at Reakar Electric India, focusing on controller validation, encoder signal processing, and model-based analysis of motor-converter systems under practical constraints — strengthening my ability to handle measurement noise, system nonlinearities, and implementation challenges.

I am currently in the final stage of my PhD (thesis submission: May 2026) and am actively seeking postdoctoral positions in power electronics, electric drives, microgrids, and energy management. My background in control design, power electronics hardware, and experimental validation equips me to contribute quickly and independently to a research group.
